Mathematical Presentation

The Kronecker cone has several interpretations that we recall below.

In Representation Theory

Given \\(s\\) partitions \\(\\lambda_0,\\dots,\\lambda_{s-1}\\) of the same integer \\(n\\), we denote by \\(g_{\\lambda_0,\\dots,\\lambda_{s-1}}\\) the multiple Kronecker coefficient, that is:

$$ g_{\\lambda_1,\\lambda_2,\\dots,\\lambda_s}=\\dim\\bigg( ([\\lambda_1]\\otimes [\\lambda_2]\\otimes\\cdots\\otimes[\\lambda_s])^{S_n} \\bigg) $$ where \\([\\lambda_i]\\) is the Spetch irreducible \\(S_n\\)-module. Fix positive integers \\(\\underline{d}=(d_0,\\dots,d_{s-1})\\). Consider $$ Kron(\\underline{d})= \\left\\{(\\lambda_1,\\dots,\\lambda_s) : \\begin{array}{l} \\lambda_i \\mbox{ are partitions such that}\\ \\ell(\\lambda_i)\\leq d_i, \\mbox{ and }\\exists k>0\\quad g_{k\\lambda_0,\\dots,k\\lambda_{s-1}}\\neq 0 \\end{array} \\right\\}. $$ So, the Kronecker cone is the asymptotic support of the Kronecker coefficients.

As a moment cone

Let \\(V_i\\) be a \\(d_i\\)-dimensional complex vector space. Then, \\(Kron(\\underline{d})\\) is the moment cone for the action of \\(\\prod_i GL(V_i)\\) on \\(V_0\\otimes\\cdots\\otimes V_{s-1}\\).

If we endow each \\(V_i\\) with an Hermitian inner product, it idenfies with the intersection of the dominant chamber and the image of the moment map for the action of \\(\\prod_i U(V_i)\\).

In terms of eigenvalues

For each \\(i\\), fix an Hermitian space \\(H_i\\) of dimension \\(d_i\\). Let \\(\\operatorname{Herm}(H_i)\\) denote the space of Hermitian endomorphisms. Given an Hermitian endomorphism \\(A\\), denote by \\(\\lambda_A\\) its spectrum (which is real) ordered in nonincreasing order (and with multiplicities).

Consider now, \\(H=H_0\\otimes\\cdots\\otimes H_{s-1}\\). Let \\(A\\in {\\operatorname{Herm}}(H)\\). Note that

$$ \\begin{array}{cl} {\\operatorname{End}}(H)&=\\bigotimes_{i=0}^{s-1} H_i^*\\otimes H_i\\ &={\\operatorname{Hom}}(H_0^*\\otimes H_0,\\bigotimes_{i=1}^{s-1} H_i^*\\otimes H_i). \\end{array} $$

Moreover, the trace gives a \\(GL(H_i)\\)-invariant map \\(tr_i:H_i^*\\otimes H_i={\\operatorname{End}}(H_i) \\longrightarrow{\\mathbb C}\\). And similarly, a map \\({\\operatorname{Tr}}:\\bigotimes_{i=1}^{s-1} H_i^*\\otimes H_i\\longrightarrow{\\mathbb C}\\). By composition, we get a \\(\\prod_i GL(V_i)\\)-equivariant map

$$ {\\operatorname{End}}(H)\\longrightarrow {\\operatorname{End}}(H_0), A\\longmapsto A_0, $$

using the \\(GL(H_0)\\)-equivariant isomorphism \\(\\operatorname{End}(H_0)^*\\simeq\\operatorname{End}(H_0)\\). Moreover, this map maps Hermitian endomorphism on Hermitian endomorphisms. Defining, similarly \\(A_i\\) for each \\(i\\), one gets the moment map

$$ \\Phi :\\operatorname{End}(H)\\longrightarrow \\prod_i\\operatorname{End}(H_i), A\\longmapsto (A_0,\\dots,A_{s-1}). $$

Let \\(\\operatorname{Herm}^{\\operatorname{rk}=1}(H)\\) denote the set of Hermitian endomorphisms of rank one. Then, Kron describes

$$ \\Phi(\\operatorname{Herm}^{\\operatorname{rk}=1}(H)) $$

in the sense that \\((A_0,\\dots,A_{s-1})\\) belongs to this image if and only if

$$ (\\lambda_{A_0},\\dots,\\lambda_{A_{s-1}})\\in Kron(\\underline{d}). $$
